PDF Remediation at Scale Pilot Submitter Name: Bohyun Kim & John Weise Written: 2/11/2026 Last Updated: 2/11/2026 1. Project Overview Goal: Evaluate the feasibility, cost, and quality of an automated PDF remediation toolchain to meet ADA Title II compliance for large-scale digital collections. This pilot aims to determine if an "automation => manual improvement" workflow is sustainable and whether the library should release auto-processed files with disclaimers or wait for future technological improvements. Deliverables: ..Assembled Test Sets: A diverse collection of PDFs including Deep Blue Docs (theses/dissertations stratified by discipline and year) and ACLS HEB Open Access titles. ..Budgetary Estimate: A ballpark dollar estimate for processing these test sets to inform long-term budgetary planning. ..Performance Evaluation Report:  ..A detailed analysis of how the toolchain handles accessibility from a human user experience perspective, including readability/listenability, going beyond technically legal Title II compliance. ..A detailed analysis of how the toolchain handles complex notations (math, music, foreign languages esp. non-latin scripts) and challenging layouts (two-column, art-heavy). ..Were the budgetary estimates for the full 3.75 million-page theses and dissertations backlog accurate based on the pilot data? ..Recommendations:  ..Insights into the speed and quality of human review/manual remediation post-automation.  ..A proposed strategy for user-facing disclaimers. Scope (In and Out): ..In Scope: Test set assembly, automated processing trials, cost analysis, and evaluation of manual remediation requirements. ..Out of Scope: Final production-level remediation of all materials in need, or development of new software features. Ideal Start Date: February 1, 2026 Ideal End Date: June 30, 2026 2. Project Justification This project directly addresses legal compliance and risk mitigation related to ADA Title II. It aligns with the library's commitment to DEIA values by seeking to provide equitable access to digital materials in a sustainable, intuitive, and welcoming manner. The pilot specifically tests if current automation can bridge the accessibility gap or if the library should wait for superior solutions. 3. Accessibility Actions The project is a foundational accessibility action designed to ensure digital content meets WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance. Key actions include validating automated output against screen readers and assistive technologies and determining the feasibility of manual remediation for complex content. 7. Risks and Mitigation Plans ..Risk: Lack of sufficient staff competency for manual remediation. ..Mitigation: The pilot will explicitly measure the "speed/quality of human review" to determine if external vendors or specialized training are required. ..Risk: A superior technological solution may emerge shortly after committing to a toolchain. ..Mitigation: Maintain an adaptive, iterative discovery mindset and include a "pivot" point in the final recommendation. ..Risk: Technical failure on high-complexity layouts (20% minority). ..Mitigation: Prioritize testing these "failure points" early in Month 1 to gauge limitations. 8.
